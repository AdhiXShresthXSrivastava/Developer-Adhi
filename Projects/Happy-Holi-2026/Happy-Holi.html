The Festival

Discover the magic behind the world's most colorful celebration

Colors

Holi celebrates the arrival of spring and the victory of good over evil. The iconic throwing of colored powders creates a mesmerizing tapestry of hues.

Bonfire

Holika Dahan on the eve of Holi symbolizes the burning of Holika, representing the triumph of truth over falsehood.

Unity

Holi breaks all barriers of caste, creed, and religion. On this day, enemies become friends, and the world dissolves into a sea of love.

Traditions

Ancient customs that make Holi special

Holika Dahan

The bonfire ritual on the night before Holi symbolizes the burning of Holika and the victory of good over evil.

Dhuleti

The main day of Holi where people throw colored powders and water at each other in celebration.

Gujiya

Special sweets. Gujiya is a popular North Indian deep-fried pastry, traditionally enjoyed during Holi and Diwali.

Family Gatherings

Families come together to apply colors, share meals, and strengthen bonds.
